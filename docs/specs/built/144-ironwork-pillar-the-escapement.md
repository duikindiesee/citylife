## Citizen case

Hennerik Vos: _"I keep the small clocks; someone keeps the big one. At midnight I hear the world get
trued. Build the pillar where the old mountain holds it. Make the climb long enough that a person
arrives quiet, and make it tall enough that Landing One knows something was here before us."_

The Pillar is a mysterious highland landmark honouring unseen work and human review. Its atmosphere
draws from the calm, constructed world of _Stargate Universe: Faith_, but its shape, mechanism,
materials, and story are original to CityLife.

## Mechanics

The monument has three funded stages, each consuming a materials/goods/treasury bundle and reserving
a five-person crew for 36 sim-hours:

1. Buried Foundation - the dais, collar, undercroft light, and roots entering the mountain.
2. Lower Monolith - the solid black shaft, pale seams, and twelve-tooth retune ring.
3. Skyline Crown - the original upper mechanism plus a visual-only 622 metre Planet Builder shaft,
   sparse distant glyphs, iris, core, and sky needle.

`pillarStage`, `pillarProgress`, `pillarBuilding`, and `lastRetuneDay` live on `ColonyState`. Stage 0
is economically and visually inert. Funding remains gated by population, surplus goods, treasury,
and available labour; a struggling colony never starts it automatically.

At stage 3 the Pillar gives the small standing unrest relief defined by
`pillarUnrestReliefPerDay`. At each 00:00 boundary, `retuneNightRelief` fires once, guarded by
`lastRetuneDay`. It has no market, wage, immigration, or production coupling.

`restingToothIndex(day, hour)`, `undercroftBarPhase(day, hour)`, and `isRetuneHour(hour)` are pure sim
functions. The renderer may smooth the ring, pulse the core, and open the iris, but smooth animation
never enters simulation state.

## Placement

The deterministic survey now targets a remote mountain shoulder rather than a low plot near Landing
One. `findIronworkPillarSite`:

- searches roughly 30 percent of the map radius, capped at 190 cells;
- enforces a substantial minimum hiking distance;
- restricts the 3x3 dais to dry, buildable, first-person-reachable ground;
- favours Highland, elevation, prominence, and adjacency to Mountain/Peak rock;
- keeps clear of road frames and existing landmarks;
- reserves the structure plus a two-cell occupied halo;
- uses deterministic score, `y`, then `x` tie-breaking.

The dais stays traversable because shared pathfinding rejects Mountain and Peak. The authored GLB's
34.8 metre summit apron, 17.2 metre structural collar, and six buried roots extend below and outside
the dais, giving the extreme shaft a substantial footing and visually joining it to the mountain.

## Hike

`buildIronworkHikePath` creates a repeatable contour path from the nearest viable colony road to the
dais. It uses the shared least-cost router with diagonal movement, beach exclusion, and a high slope
penalty. Reserved land is avoided where possible, with a terrain-only fallback for older developed
saves.

The route is a footpath, not a road. It does not enter `roadKind` and is not drivable. The R3F layer
renders terrain-following shoulder and worn-tread ribbons, irregular centre stones, and paired
waystones with pale night slits. Foliage is cleared along the tread and around the destination.

## Render contract

The active implementation is `src/colony/render/R3FIronworkPillar.tsx`, mounted in the dressing stage
of `R3FPlanetRenderer`. The obsolete `PlanetRenderer`/`ironworkPillarProps` handoff is superseded.

The source asset is `public/assets/citylife/props/ironwork-pillar.glb`, regenerated by
`scripts/generate_ironwork_pillar.mjs`. Required named nodes include:

- `Pillar_Stage_1`, `Pillar_Stage_2`, `Pillar_Stage_3`
- `Pillar_Summit_Apron`, `Pillar_Obsidian_Table`, `Pillar_Dais_Rune_01`, `Pillar_Sentinel_01`
- `Pillar_Distant_Monolith`, `Pillar_Sky_Glyph_01`
- `Pillar_Retune_Ring`
- `Pillar_Crown_Iris`, `Pillar_Iris_Left`, `Pillar_Iris_Right`
- `Pillar_Crown_Core`, `Pillar_Crown_Halo`

The GLB is a solid tapered monolith, not a see-through gantry. It is 622 metres high, contains 86
mesh resources across 93 mesh nodes, uses seven PBR materials, and has no images, textures, cameras,
or lights. The original lower
64 metres retain gameplay collision and the reachable summit; the extreme upper shaft is a visual
setpiece so platformer movement and level readability do not change. Emissive seams, summit runes,
retune mechanism, distant glyphs, and crown are driven by the existing world bloom.

At stage 0 the model and trail are absent. A building stage grows with `pillarProgress`; completed
stages stay at full scale. At stage 3 midnight, the iris opens, the pale light rises, and the ring
free-runs before returning to its deterministic resting tooth.
